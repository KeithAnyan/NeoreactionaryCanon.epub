A Brief History of Reactionary Taxonomy

In April and May of 2013, neoreaction experienced what Nick Land would later dub a “Cambrian Explosion.” Scharlach explains:

It’s a sign of maturity that the neoreactionary movement has begun to go “meta” on itself. Scholars of the Dark Enlightenment are beginning to ask self-reflexive questions about who they are, where divisions lie, and what it all means.

Well, we in the Carlyle Club for Young Reactionaries (Students Against a Democratic Society) aren’t qualified to know what any of it means, but we think this is all very exciting and we insist on being a part of it anyway! So we spent all night learning.

The story so far…

  • Veteran right-winger Anomaly UK writes a concise introduction to neoreaction.
  • James Donald defines Dark Enlightenment and contemplates a restoration.
  • Spandrell divides the new reaction into three branches: traditionalist, nationalist, and capitalist.

Meanwhile, Scipio Germanicus launches The Reactionary Thinker.

  • Nick Land relabels the trichotomy: theonomist, ethno-nationalist, and techno-commercialist.
  • Frost comes up with three red sub-pills of his own: Christian, secular, and nihilistic.
  • Anomaly UK contemplates reactionary unity in light of Spandrell’s trichotomy.

It is at this point that the Carlyle Club decides that what we really need right now are wizards. And dragons. And trolls. So many trolls. Heroes Arise!

We classify our Heroes by broad approach to Dark Enlightenment:

  1. Formalists: theory of government
  2. Futurists: technology & transhumanism
  3. Scientists: impeccably empirical
  4. Racialists: diversity sucks
  5. Masculinists: man up already
  6. Traditionalists: keep the faith

You can identify each Hero’s type by the icon in the top left corner. There is also a special university icon for collaborative efforts, and a bag of gold for Heroes who were sponsored by a donor! (A donation of $100 gets you a card for anyone you like.)
